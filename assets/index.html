Frequently Asked Questions

Bolt 6 LT offers compact design with 3G-SDI (1080p) and HDMI (4K30). Bolt 6 XT, with a more robust processor,
provides 12G-SDI (4K60), 3D LUTs, Low Power Mode, video scaling, frame-
rate conversion, anamorphic
desqueeze, and SDI troubleshooting. XT also has two extra
transmitter antennas for better connection and image
fidelity in tough RF environments.

Yes, Bolt 6 uses AES-256 encryption, a military-grade standard that ensures only authorized receivers can
decode the video signal. Bolt 6 RXs are securely paired to the TX, meaning only those devices can communicate
together. This prevents unauthorized access.

Yes, Bolt 6 can be controlled using the Bolt App, which simplifies device pairing, allows fine-tuned frequency
adjustments, and includes a built-in spectrum analyzer for advanced RF diagnostics.

Bolt 6 is fully compatible with all Bolt 4K models, as they all use the BB3 wireless platform. This platform
supports zero-delay 4K transmission, wireless camera control, and other advanced features. Legacy Bolt
products built on the BB2 platform are not compatible with Bolt 6.
